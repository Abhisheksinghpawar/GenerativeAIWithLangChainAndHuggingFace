===============
ROADMAP FOR NLP
===============

1) Foundations
    - Core libraries: NLTK, spaCy, scikit-learn
    - Basics: Python, data handling, evaluation metrics

2) Text Preprocessing (Cleaning & normalization)
    - Tokenization: paragraph → sentences → words
    - Lowercasing, punctuation removal, normalization
    - Stopword removal, stemming vs. lemmatization
    - Handling rare words, misspellings, and normalization

3) Classical Feature Extraction
    - Bag of Words (BoW): simple count vectors
    - TF–IDF: importance-weighted features
    - n-grams: unigrams, bigrams (and higher-order n-grams)

4) Word Embeddings (Dense representations)
    - Word2Vec, GloVe, FastText
    - Averaging word vectors for sentence-level features
    - Contextual embeddings (intro): ELMo, contextualized representations

5) Deep Learning for Sequences
    - RNN basics and limitations
    - LSTM and GRU: handling long-range dependencies
    - Use cases: sequence classification, tagging, generation

6) Transformers & Attention
    - Transformer architecture: self-attention, positional encodings
    - Encoder/decoder usage for different tasks

7) Pretrained Models & Fine-tuning
    - BERT and variants (RoBERTa, DistilBERT, etc.)
    - Fine-tune for classification, NER, QA, summarization

8) Common NLP Tasks / Applications
    - Text classification, sentiment analysis
    - Named Entity Recognition (NER)
    - Machine translation, summarization, question answering

9) Practical Learning Path (suggested)
    - Practice preprocessing and feature extraction on small datasets
    - Train classical models (logistic regression, SVM) with TF–IDF
    - Train simple word2vec, experiment with embeddings
    - Build RNN/LSTM classifier, then try Transformer-based fine-tuning
    - Apply models to a small end-to-end project (text classifier or NER)

10) Resources
    - Tutorials: official spaCy, TensorFlow, PyTorch docs
    - Papers: "Attention Is All You Need", BERT paper
    - Libraries: Hugging Face Transformers for pretrained models

Keep this as a living roadmap — add links, project ideas, and notes.

TOKENIZATION IN NLP
===================

Corpus - Paragraph
Documents - Sentence
Vocabulary - Unique words in the paragraph
Words - All words in a Corpus

Tokenization - 

"My name is Abhishek and I have a interst in teching ML, NLP and DL. I am also a youtuber"

    - This whole is a Corpus

Tokens {Sentences or documents}

Simple process in which paragraph is converted to sentences. Looks for fulls top and exclaimation

     - My name is Abhishek and I have a interst in teaching ML, NLP and DL
     - I am also a youtuber

Words can also be a token - Sentences can also be a token
